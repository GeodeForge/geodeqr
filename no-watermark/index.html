Nothing you type reaches us. There's nowhere for it to go — no server, no database, no backend. Open your browser's Network tab and make a code: nothing happens. Read the source.

    
Why editing the image doesn't work

    
A dynamic code doesn't contain your link — it contains a link to the generator's server, which forwards each scan to your link. Scrub the watermark off and reprint it, and your scans still route through that server, and the code still dies if the forwarding stops. The watermark was never the part that could hurt you.

  


  

    
Why there's none here

    
There's no watermark here for the same reason there's no "Pro" button: no paid tier exists. Nothing is held back, so there's nothing to sell back to you — and a watermark with nothing to unlock would just be a stain on your code.

  


  

    
Vector export, free — and it keeps your design

    
The formats print shops ask for — SVG, PDF and EPS — are all here, free, and the export keeps your design: colors, styling, labels.

    
The PDF's code matrix is true vector, crisp at any size. The labels are embedded at print DPI as raster, on purpose: PDF's built-in fonts are Latin-only, and vector text would silently mangle a Chinese or Arabic label. A pretty export that destroys your shop's name in its own language is not a feature. Also free: print-DPI PNG, transparent backgrounds, and JPG for systems that insist.

  


  

    
What we don't have, and why that's the deal

    
No dynamic codes, no scan analytics. Those genuinely need a server running for as long as your code lives, plus someone moderating what gets redirected — real costs, honestly sold by the providers who charge for them. If that's what you need, buy it knowingly; expect an account, a subscription, and probably a watermark on the free tier, because that funnel is what pays for the server.

    
Declining all of that is not a gap in this tool. It's the reason everything else on this page can be free with nothing held back.

  


  

    
Before it goes to print

    
    
      
  • Use a vector format — SVG or PDF — for anything bigger than a sticker. Pixels have a size; maths doesn't.
    • 
      
  • Test the printed one, not the screen. Print at 100%, scan it with a cheap phone in bad light.
    • 
      
  • Read it back before the print run. Ten seconds now, or a reprint later.
